Decide How Much House You Can Afford

A mortgage preapproval is important because it will help you decide how much house you can afford. The best place to start is your desired monthly payment and to work backwards from there. Your mortgage consultant will come up with a purchase price based on this monthly mortgage payment and the down payment you have set aside to contribute.

How much can you borrow to purchase a home?

The maximum home price you can purchase is determined by calculating your current monthly payments listed on your credit report divided by your gross monthly income. This called your debt-to-income ratio, also referred to as your DTI. Your day-to-day expenses are not included in this ratio. Only payment obligations such as car payments, student loans, and minimum credit card payments are included. Each loan type and lender has slightly different thresholds for how much you can borrow, but they are all similar.

What other factors are considered in your mortgage approval?

In addition to your DTI, several other factors are considered, like your job history. Two years without any significant employment gaps is desired. The good news is education can count towards your work history.

Your credit score and credit history are other important factors. Mortgage lenders give more favorable interest rates to borrowers with higher credit scores, meaning you can borrow slightly more. The ideal credit score is anything over a 760. However, borrowers with scores as low as 640 can be preapproved for a mortgage loan. Therefore, it’s recommended to meet with a mortgage consultant as early as a year before you plan on buying a home in case there are any improvements you need to make to increase your credit score.

When does your mortgage approval expire?

Your mortgage approval usually expires 90 days from when your credit report was initially pulled. At that point, your mortgage consultant will need to re-run your credit and obtain updated income and asset information, like pay stubs and bank statements.
