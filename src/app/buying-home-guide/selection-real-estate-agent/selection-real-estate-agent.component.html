How to Find the Right Real Estate Agent

If you’re buying a home, then there is no cost to work with a real estate agent. Their commission of 3% for the buyer’s agent and 3% for the seller’s agent comes directly from the seller. The first place to start your agent hunt is by asking for recommendations from friends and family. If someone you know and trust had a great experience with their real estate agent, chances are good that you will too.

Once you’ve received a few names, take time to interview the agents to see who will best fit your home buying experience. Below are a few, key questions to ask during the agent interview process.

Real Estate Agent Interview Questions:
  • • How long have you been selling real estate? In this industry, having an experienced agent on your side is helpful.
  • • How many homes did you help clients buy and sell last year? A realtor who only sold four or five homes does not have the best track record and may only be working part-time. The best agents close on several homes a month and are successful for a reason.
  • • Can you provide me with the contact information of your three most recent sales? Asking for the three most recent home sales ensures the agent isn’t just handpicking clients whom they know will give the most favorable testimony.
  • • What is your availability for showing houses? If you are only available to preview homes for sale on evenings and weekends, make sure your agent’s schedule is conducive to that.
  • • Which neighborhoods are you most familiar with? A realtor who doesn’t sell many homes in your desired neighborhoods may not be familiar with pricing and the neighborhood’s personality — is it more geared toward empty-nesters, young professionals, or young families? Do most children attend public or private schools? Is local government capable of operating efficiently to maintain roads and schools?
