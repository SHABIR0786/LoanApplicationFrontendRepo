What You Need for Online Mortgage Preapproval

A free mortgage consultation will include a review of your credit to pinpoint areas in which you could improve. We recommend starting with one before you begin visiting open houses and meeting with realtors. You’ll want to know if you qualify for a mortgage and your budget before you find your dream home.

When should you meet with a mortgage consultant?

If you are a first-time home buyer or haven’t purchased a home in a long time, meeting with a mortgage consultant a year before you plan on buying a home is the best way to prepare your finances for the mortgage preapproval process.

How can a mortgage consultation help you prepare to buy a home?

You may need to keep your credit card balances lower, open a small installment loan, or pay off debt to be approved for the home purchase price you desire. If you’re thinking of buying a new car within the year, you may need to wait until you close on your home to ensure there is room in your debt-to-income ratio. A free mortgage consultation will put you in the best financial place to buy a house.

When should you finalize your mortgage preapproval?

When you are three to six months out from starting your house hunt with an agent, it’s time to gather the required documentation and sit down with your mortgage broker again to finalize your preapproval. In general, a mortgage lender will want to review the following most recent information:

  • • Two years of your tax documentation
  • • A month of paystubs
  • • Sixty days of current bank statements

At this time, you will also need to let the mortgage consultant know the course of your down payment funds, especially if gift funds are involved or if you are selling stocks or bonds. You’ll need a documented paper trail of these transactions.
