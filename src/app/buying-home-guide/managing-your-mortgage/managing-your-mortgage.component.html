Evaluate Your Mortgage Annually

The work isn’t done once you close on your mortgage. Each year there are times when you will need to make decisions. You’ll need to evaluate your current mortgage and make sure it’s still the best fit for your financial goals.

  • Making extra mortgage payments each year can save you
    thousands on your loan.
  • It can also help you pay off your mortgage sooner.
  • Finally, you’ll pay less interest as a result.
  • Each year your mortgage servicer will review your escrow
    statement – part of your bill from which taxes and homeowner’s
    insurance is paid.
  • If your tax assessment or homeowner’s insurance increases, your
    mortgage payment increased to cover this cost.
  • You will also have the option to pay the difference in full and avoid
    an increase in your payment.
  • The principal and interest portion of your payment is fixed if you
    have a fixed rate mortgage.
  • You will receive a 1098 form in the mail each year from your
    servicer, which includes the interest and mortgage expenses you
    incurred over the year.
  • If it is the year in which you closed, you will also want to use your
    settlement statement to account for expenses paid in that year.
  • Depending on your financial situation, these fees may be used as
    deductions to reduce your taxable income.
  • Reach back out to your mortgage consultant annually to complete
    a review of your mortgage.
  • If your home value appreciated significantly, and a new appraisal
    and refinance might remove your monthly mortgage insurance
    premium saving you money.
  • Rates may have decreased. Refinancing to lower rate and term
    could benefit you.
  • Be sure to check out our refinance guide for more information.
