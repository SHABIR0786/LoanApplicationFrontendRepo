How to Place a Fair and Competitive Bid

There are so many factors that contribute to a competitive home purchase offer, and money isn’t the only piece being considered by the seller.

  • Decide on your offer price.
  • Analyze price per square foot and how many days the home has been
    on the market.
  • Determine whether your offer is competitive.
  • Be flexible, especially if you decide your home budget is tight or you
    didn’t get the mortgage preapproval.
  • Learn what closing date the seller prefers
  • Find out if the seller desires to close and rent back from you while
    arranging to move into their next house.
  • If your lease is going to expire, consider moving in with friends or
    family to make it work. Or store your furniture and rent an Airbnb
    until it’s time to move in.
  • Alternatively, you might consider lowering your down payment to
    avoid asking the seller for closing costs. This assumes it doesn’t
    impact your mortgage pre-approval.
  • Have a home loan pre-approval letter from a local, reputable lender.
  • Give your mortgage consultant permission to speak to the seller and
    seller’s agent about the strength of your mortgage preapproval.
  • Once you’ve sent in your offer, it will be accepted, rejected or countered. If countered, you need to
    think carefully about the maximum purchase price and closing terms that will work for you. Remember,
    buying a home is a long-term financial commitment. It’s okay to walk away if you believe you would be paying too much for a home.
