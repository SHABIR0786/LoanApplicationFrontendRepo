Close on Your Mortgage Refinance Loan

Talk to your lender about ways to finance closing costs with a refinance loan. You can usually finance these into conventional loans or pay them with a rebate by selecting a slightly higher interest rate. If you can pay for closing costs this way, you may not owe much on the day of closing, if anything.

  • Who needs to be present at the closing?

    Any borrower on the loan needs to be present at the closing. If you cannot be present, you may be able to arrange for a power of attorney to sign for you. You must make sure your lender is aware if you plan on using a power of attorney.

  • What should I bring to closing?

    Bring your photo ID and a certified check for any funds due at closing (unless the funds are being wired).

  • Will I receive the funds for a cash out refinance at closing?

    No, you will not receive the funds until 3-5 days after your refinance closes. Federal regulation requires lenders to give you a three-day waiting period after closing to terminate the refinance if you reconsider.
