Refinancing your mortgage loan is a big undertaking and a significant financial decision. Choosing the right lender will set the tone for a positive experience and outcome. Here are a few questions to ask when selecting a lender:

  • Can I apply for the refinance online?

    Completing an online mortgage application around your schedule and in the comfort of your own home or office is ideal. We provide a user-friendly online form to make this part of the process quick and easy.

  • How long will it take to close on my loan?

    Some lenders can take up to 60 days to close a mortgage refinance. At EZ Online Mortgage, our refinances often close between 30 and 45 days.

  • What’s your availability?

    Work with a lender who is available when your schedule allows time for a phone conference or meeting.

  • What are your fees and rates?

    The cost of processing fees, appraisals, and interest rates vary from lender to lender. You can ask for a loan estimate to compare these costs across different lenders. Keep in mind, until your interest rate is locked, it’s just an estimate. Also, the lowest interest rate might not always be the best experience.

There are many costs associated with a refinance. The typical refinance costs 2-3 percent of the total loan amount. Here is a breakdown of what you can expect to pay:

Application/Processing Fee
  • Every lender has a base fee. (It may be called an application fee, a processing fee, or etc.).
  • This fee is typically collected at closing.
Appraisal Fee
  • Appraisal fees vary from lender to lender.
  • This fee is typically collected upfront from the borrower.
Attorney/Settlement Agent Fee
  • Your lender will provide you with an estimate for these fees.
  • You will not know the exact amount until you have selected your settlement agent.
  • If you don’t select an agent, your lender can recommend a reputable agent in your area.
Title Insurance Fee
  • This fee involves a title search of your property’s ownership and any liens that may have been placed on the property.
  • It also includes insurance to protect you and the lender from any future issues with the ownership of the property.
  • Title insurance protects you from potentially tens of thousands of dollars in legal fees.
