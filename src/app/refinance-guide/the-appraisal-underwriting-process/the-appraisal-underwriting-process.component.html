Your Mortgage Refinance Appraisal

Once your appraisal value is received, you and your mortgage lender may have some adjustments to make in the refinance scenario to finalize the loan amount. The appraisal is one of the most important pieces of a refinance. So, it’s important to research recent sales in your neighborhood before you start your refinance. Your realtor may be able with an MLS search and send you recent sales from the neighborhood. This allows you to review some comparison sales the appraiser may be using.

  • What if my appraisal comes in low?

    If it comes in so low that you need to add mortgage insurance and a refinance no longer makes sense, you can cancel the refinance. You may also need to cancel the mortgage if your loan-to-value no longer qualifies you for a refinance. Canceling the refinance is an unfortunate outcome and you will not be refunded the appraisal fee. If you have plenty of equity in your home already, a low home appraisal may not result in any issues with your loan.

  • What if my appraisal comes in higher than anticipated?

    A higher than estimated appraisal is great news, but it won’t affect your refinance unless it eliminates the need for mortgage insurance.

Once you submit all your documentation and your appraisal has been received, your loan file will be sent to underwriting. Just as in a purchase, the underwriter will do an initial review and issue a conditional approval. Once you and your mortgage lender gather the documents requested by the mortgage lender, the underwriter will issue a final approval and you’re one step closer to closing.
