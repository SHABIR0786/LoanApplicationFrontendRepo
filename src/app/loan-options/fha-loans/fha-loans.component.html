FHA Loan

Loans guaranteed by the FHA (Federal Housing Administration) may be a great choice for borrowers with smaller down payments, but good credit scores.

Is an FHA
right for me

An FHA (Federal Housing Administration) Loan could be the best product for you if you have a good credit score, money for at least a 3.5% down payment, and meet other qualifications

FHA loans are guaranteed by the Department of Housing and Urban Development, and the FHA approves the lenders that offer these loans. Almost 50 million borrowers have received FHA-insured loans since the program started in 1934. FHA loans are sometimes called first-time home buyers because of the easygoing credit standards and low down payments.

What are the advantages
of an FHA loan

The low down payment and lenient credit score requirements are among the attractive aspects of an FHA loan from the buyer’s perspective. For the lender, the federal guarantee makes the loans secure.

The borrowers must be buying the home as a primary residence, not investment property, which makes the loan a higher prospect for repayment. FHA loans also allow borrowers to use gifted funds for both down payments and closing costs in many cases, making these loans even more accessible. The FHA program offers fixed rate and adjustable loans, as well as Jumbo products.

How do I qualify
for an FHA loan

To qualify for an FHA loan, you must be a legal U.S. resident with a Social Security number and intend to live in the home you are purchasing. The target property will need to appraise for at least the proposed purchase price to qualify.

As a borrower, you will need a credit score of 580 to obtain a loan with the 3.5% down payment. If your score is lower, you may be able to get an FHA loan, but with a larger down payment. Individual lenders may have higher credit score requirements.

Why choose an FHA loan?

  • Choose an FHA loan if you don’t have savings for a large down payment.
  • Choose an FHA loan if you need a co-applicant who won’t be living with you.
  • Choose an FHA loan if you have a seller willing to offset some of the sales price.
  • Choose an FHA loan if you have a stable employment history.

How Does Mortgage Insurance Work

You’ll have to pay primary mortgage insurance (PMI) if your down payment is less than 20%.

  • Mortgage insurance is required on almost every new loan with a down payment of less than twenty percent.
  • Mortgage insurance protects the lender from losses, and this allows them to offer loans they might not otherwise.
  • Most conventional loans qualify for private mortgage insurance, paid monthly along with the principal and interest.
  • In some cases with a conventional loan, you can request to have the mortgage insurance dropped when your equity in the loan reaches twenty percent, but in other cases you may need to refinance the loan.
  • FHA loans have mortgage insurance that you pay to the Federal Housing Administration. For these loans there is a fee at the loan closing plus a monthly charge.
  • FHA loans must maintain the mortgage insurance, so to stop paying the MI, you need to refinance into a conventional loan type.
  • VA (Veteran’s Administration) loans don’t need mortgage insurance, because the VA guarantees the loan for you. But there is an upfront funding fee that varies depending on several factors, including:
  • Your service type
  • Your down payment
  • Whether you are buying or refinancing
  • Whether you are disabled
  • If this is your first VA loan
