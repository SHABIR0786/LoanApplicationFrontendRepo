Why choose a VA loan?

  • Choose a VA loan to take advantage of one of the benefits of serving your country.
  • Choose a VA loan to get up to 100% financing with no down payment.
  • Choose a VA loan to get up to 100% financing with no mortgage insurance.
  • Choose a VA loan to eliminate the chance of a prepayment penalty.
  • Choose a VA loan for lower closing costs than some other loan types.

How Does Mortgage Insurance Work?

You’ll have to pay primary mortgage insurance (PMI) if your down payment is less than 20%.

  • Mortgage insurance is required on almost every new loan with a down payment of less than twenty percent.
  • Mortgage insurance protects the lender from losses, and this allows them to offer loans they might not otherwise.
  • Most conventional loans qualify for private mortgage insurance, paid monthly along with the principal and interest.
  • In some cases with a conventional loan, you can request to have the mortgage insurance dropped when your equity in the loan reaches twenty percent, but in other cases you may need to refinance the loan. loan.
  • FHA loans have mortgage insurance that you pay to the Federal Housing Administration. For these loans there is a fee at the loan closing plus a monthly charge.
  • FHA loans must maintain the mortgage insurance, so to stop paying the MI, you need to refinance into a conventional loan type.
  • VA (Veteran’s Administration) loans don’t need mortgage insurance, because the VA VA (Veteran’s Administration) loans don’t need mortgage insurance, because the VA guarantees the loan for you. But there is an upfront funding fee that varies depending on several factors, including:
