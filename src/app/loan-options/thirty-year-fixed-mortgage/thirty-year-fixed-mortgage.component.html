Is a 30 year fixed rate loan
right for me?

The 30 year fixed rate loan is always among the most popular choices for borrowers. If you need lower payments, and the security of an interest rate that won’t change, the 30-year fixed rate may be the right loan for you.

If you are making a down payment of 20% or more, you won’t need private mortgage insurance. The federal government doesn’t guarantee conventional loans (30-year loans are one of several types of loan that fall into the conventional loan category), but it does set the limits for how much a conforming loan can be. The Federal Housing Finance Agency sets a baseline limit. In 2021 that limit is $548,250. Some parts of the country have higher limits, since housing prices are higher. A maximum conforming loan amount in these areas can reach $822,375.

What are the advantages of
a 30 year fixed loan?

A 30-year fixed rate mortgage may offer the best way for a borrower to “buy more house.” Because the length of the loan is longer, the payments are lower than with some other loan types.

This allows you to manage a higher loan payment or leave some room in your budget for other things. This loan product is ideal for a borrower who plans to stay in the home for a long period and doesn’t want the risk of a rising interest rate.

How do I qualify for A 30-Year
Fixed Loan?

To get approved for a 30-year fixed rate mortgage, you need a good credit score, proof of income, and money saved for a down paymen0t. You should also have a debt-to-income ratio of 50% or less. Debt-to-income ratio is the amount of your monthly income that is going to debts like credit cards, car loans, and student loans.

Why choose a 30-year fixed rate loan?

  • Choose a 30-year fixed rate mortgage if you want to make the same payment each month.
  • Choose a 30-year fixed rate mortgage if you want to spread the loan out for the longest period, for the lowest payment.
  • Choose this loan type if you plan to stay in the same home for a long time.
  • Your interest rate is fixed for the life of the loan, so you don’t have worry about rates rising.
  • Choose this loan type if you don’t anticipate large income increases in the future.
  • You may be able to refinance this loan later if things change.

How Does Mortgage Insurance Work

You’ll have to pay primary mortgage insurance (PMI) if your down payment is less than 20%.

  • Mortgage insurance is required on almost every new loan with a down payment of less than twenty percent.
  • Mortgage insurance protects the lender from losses, and this allows them to offer loans they might not otherwise.
  • Most conventional loans qualify for private mortgage insurance, paid monthly along with the principal and interest.
  • In some cases with a conventional loan, you can request to have the mortgage insurance dropped when your equity in the loan reaches twenty percent, but in other cases you may need to refinance the loan.
  • FHA loans have mortgage insurance that you pay to the Federal Housing Administration. For these loans there is a fee at the loan closing plus a monthly charge.
  • FHA loans must maintain the mortgage insurance, so to stop paying the MI, you need to refinance into a conventional loan type.
  • VA (Veteran’s Administration) loans don’t need mortgage insurance, because the VA guarantees the loan for you. But there is an upfront funding fee that varies depending on several factors, including:
  • Your service type
  • Your down payment
  • Whether you are buying or refinancing
  • Whether you are disabled
  • If this is your first VA loan
