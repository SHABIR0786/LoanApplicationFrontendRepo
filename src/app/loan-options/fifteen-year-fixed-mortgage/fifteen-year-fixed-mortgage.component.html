Is a 15 year fixed rate loan
right for me?

A 15-year fixed rate mortgage loan might be the best choice for you if you prefer the security of a stable interest rate, but you would like to make progress toward paying off your loan more quickly than with a 30-year mortgage.

The monthly payments will be higher than the payments for the same loan amount on a 30-year schedule, but the total you pay to interest will be much less over the life of the loan. 15-year fixed rate loans are conventional mortgages, just like 30-year loans, so the loan amount limits are the same. Conventional loans have these limits because the lender you get the loan from usually sells it to a GSE (government sponsored enterprise like Freddie Mac or Fannie Mae) which in turn sells it to investors. This lets the bank or other lender offer mortgages to more borrowers.

What are the advantages of
a 15 year fixed loan?

The biggest advantage for you is paying less interest while you pay the loan. By limiting your loan term to 15 years,
you dramatically reduce the amount of interest you pay.

The 15-year loan will almost always have a slightly lower interest rate than a 30-year loan, and with the shorter term, you would pay less than half the amount of interest over the course of the loan. You will also increase your equity in the home faster, because you are paying more toward the principal balance each month. This is helpful in case you need to refinance.

How do I qualify for
A 15-Year Fixed Loan?

For the conventional loan, whether 15-year or 30-year fixed rate, the key factors in qualifying are your credit score and debt-to-income ratio. There are two versions of the ratio that lenders may use: front-end and back-end.

Either type compares the percentage of your monthly income that is committed. A front-end ratio considers just your housing costs, while the back-end ratio (more common in evaluating you for a mortgage approval) includes other debt payments as well, and usually needs to be under 43 %.

Why choose a 15-year fixed rate loan?

  • Choose a 15-year loan if you want to pay your mortgage off more quickly.
  • Choose a 15-year loan if you prefer to pay higher monthly amounts but less interest overall.
  • Choose a 15-year loan if you plan to pay the mortgage off to retire.
  • Choose a 15-year loan if you want protection from changes in rates but a lower interest rate.

How Does Mortgage Insurance Work

  • Mortgage insurance is required on almost every new loan with a down payment of less than twenty percent.
  • Mortgage insurance protects the lender from losses, and this allows them to offer loans they might not otherwise.
  • Most conventional loans qualify for private mortgage insurance, paid monthly along with the principal and interest.
  • In some cases with a conventional loan, you can request to have the mortgage insurance dropped when your equity in the loan reaches twenty percent, but in other cases you may need to refinance the loan.
  • FHA loans have mortgage insurance that you pay to the Federal Housing Administration. For these loans there is a fee at the loan closing plus a monthly charge.
  • FHA loans must maintain the mortgage insurance, so to stop paying the MI, you need to refinance into a conventional loan type.
  • VA (Veteran’s Administration) loans don’t need mortgage insurance, because the VA guarantees the loan for you. But there is an upfront funding fee that varies depending on several factors, including:
  • Your service type
  • Your down payment
  • Whether you are buying or refinancing
  • Whether you are disabled
  • If this is your first VA loan
